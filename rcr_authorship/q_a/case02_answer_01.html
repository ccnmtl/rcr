1. What types of conflict of interest might arise when someone is asked to review a paper or grant application?

The reviewer may benefit or not suffer financially from the findings in the paper or grant application; may be too close to the author or applicant; may not like the author or applicant; may disagree with the methodology or science of the author or applicant; may be in very clear and direct competition with the author or applicant; or may have religious or philosophical views that are in conflict with the research of the author or applicant.
