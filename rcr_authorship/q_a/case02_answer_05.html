5. What are some of the challenges in the current peer-review process, in which the peer reviewer is anonymous but the author is known to the reviewer?

Some say that anonymous peer reviewers can abuse their position by holding up the review process to finish their own work, by appropriating information, or by giving bad reviews to disliked colleagues-actions that the author might be able to detect if he or she knew who the reviewer was. One new approach to peer review makes the author anonymous to the reviewer. Another approach has both the author and reviewer aware of each other’s identity.
