3. Should Dr. Rolands point out to the editor of the first journal his potential conflict of interest?

Editors, like authors and peer reviewers, also have potential conflicts of interest that could influence whether they accept or reject a paper. In this case, the editor seemed to be a personal friend of Drs. Jones and Marcus and may be compromised. Dr. Rolands might want to consider writing the editor and asking for a re-review with another editor.
