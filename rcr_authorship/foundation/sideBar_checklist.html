»Checklist for Authors«

Given that there are no universal standards for authorship, Kathy Barker, writing in Science’s Next Wave in 2002, offers a good checklist for authors:

  • Have all authors contributed to the work?
  • Is the work true and unpublished?
  • Are the contributions of those who helped with the paper, but whose contributions don’t rise to the level of authors, listed in the acknowledgments section?
  • Have all people described in the acknowledgments, and all people listed in personal communications, been notified?
  • Have all authors read the paper prior to submission and been given an opportunity to comment? Have their comments been dealt with to their satisfaction?
  • Have all potential conflicts of interest been disclosed?
  • Have all protocols for the use of human subjects and animals been observed?
  • Is the literature review balanced? Does it cite publications of rival groups and those which present opposing theories?
  • Have you retained raw data associated with the paper? (There is no legal limit on the amount of time data must be kept, although five to seven years is suggested.)
  • Are you personally convinced that the data are sound?
  • Keep all correspondence with journals for at least six months after the paper is accepted for publication.
