Graduate-student authorship issues: In different academic environments, including psychology, sociology, and the biomedical sciences, graduate students may not get credit as an author for the research and writing they do, even if they participated significantly in the design, execution, and writing of the work. The policy usually depends on the adviser. If a faculty member is up for tenure, he or she may want to be listed as a single or lead author on a paper, even if the student did much of the work. Students should be aware of the policy of their adviser.
