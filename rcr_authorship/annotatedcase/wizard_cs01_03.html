Taking responsibility: If your name is on a paper, how much responsibility do you have for the work of the co-authors who are also listed? While it may not be possible for one person to have a complete understanding of the nuances of every aspect of a paper, several co-authors may have to share the accountability, with each providing expertise in a particular area. Also, senior investigators need to be aware of the work of junior colleagues; recent cases of fraud have involved graduate students, postdoctoral fellows, and junior scientists reporting results that neither the supervisor nor the peer-review process detected.
