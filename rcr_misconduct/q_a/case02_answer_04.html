4. How else do you think the study design might have been improved to prevent potential fabrication or falsification?

Surveys and tests often have questions that are asked in different ways, so-called internal controls, to make sure that information collected in one place is similar to that collected elsewhere. Dr. Robert Shapiro, professor of political science at Columbia University, says that one lesson in this case study is that data collectors should be kept insulated from the study's purpose so they do not know the consequences of right or wrong answers.
