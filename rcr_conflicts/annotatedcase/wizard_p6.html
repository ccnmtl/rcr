Are you aware of a 1996 survey by David Blumenthal, revealing that "20% of life-science faculty admitted to withholding data from publication for more than six months to protect the commercial value of the results?" Putting restrictions on the publication of results may be good for the sponsor’s stock price, but is not consistent with the free exchange of ideas that is so important to the scientific enterprise and may even cause harm if important research results involving human subjects is not reported in a timely manner.
