David Rothman, PhD, is the Bernard Schoenberg Professor of Social Medicine, and Professor of History at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, and Director of the Center for the Study of Society and Medicine. David Rothman is particularly concerned with the issues of professional medical ethics. He is the author of several books including: The Discovery of the Asylum, which was a co-winner of the Albert J. Beveridge Prize from the American Historical Association; Conscience and Convenience; the Willowbrook Wars; Strangers at the Bedside: A History of How Law and Bioethics Transformed Medical Decision Making; and Beginnings Count: The Technological Imperative in American Health Care. He chairs the advisory board of the Program on Medicine as a Profession, organized under the auspices of the Open Society Institute. He also serves on the Board of Trustees of the Open Society Institute-New York.
